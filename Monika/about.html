About Sindhudurg

Sindhudurg is a coastal district located in the southwestern part of Maharashtra, India. Established on May 1, 1981, Sindhudurg is known for its scenic beauty, historic forts, and vibrant cultural heritage.

The district is named after the Sindhudurg Fort, a significant historical fortification built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in the 17th century. This fort, located on an island in the Arabian Sea, is a symbol of the Maratha empire's naval power and strategic prowess.

Sindhudurg is renowned for its lush greenery, pristine beaches, and serene backwaters. Key attractions include Tarkarli Beach, known for its clear waters and water sports, and the historic Vengurla Fort. The district also boasts several temples, natural reserves, and vibrant local markets that reflect the rich culture of the region.
